Israel-Palestine

 

Both Israel and Palestine are now in an existential crisis. After 75 years, neither the Jews nor the Arabs have been able to provide for themselves with efficient governance or effective defense.

 

1)     Israel views Gaza as an illegal and lawless territory that seceded from the Palestinian Authority and is controlled by the terrorist group Hamas.

2)     As such, the Oslo Accords between Israel and PLO, no longer apply it to it.

3)     Israel intends to liberate Gaza from Hamas

4)     A joined temporary trusteeship of Israel and the Palestinian Authority (Trusteeship) is proclaimed as a lawful government of Gaza, tasked with the welfare and protection of its population

5)     As the Trusteeship acquires control over Gaza territory, it reserves the right to evacuate Gazans to safer locations of the Trusteeship, as it deems necessary and appropriate

6)     When the liberation is complete, the Trusteeship will be transformed into a permanent political structure, with a Constitution established by the Trusteeship. This structure will be called Israel-Palestine (IP). The present states of Israel and Palestine will cease to exist.

7)     The borders between Gaza and Israel as well as Israel and the West Bank will be abolished.

8)     IP will adhere to all international treaties to which Israel currently is a party.

9)     IP will originally not be a member of the United Nations. Its interest in joining the UN will be determined at a later date.

10)  The UN agencies, including UNRWA, will not operate on IP territory without its explicit and possibly time-bound authorization.

11)  The population of IP will be subdivided into citizens, permanent residents and temporary residents. Citizens of Israel will become citizens of IP, and permanent residents become permanent residents of IP. The remaining individuals will be classified as temporary or permanent residents, depending on the level of self-sufficiency and integration into the IP society. The rights of all these groups will be constitutionally protected.

12)  Path to citizenship will be offered to permanent residents, as well as path to permanent residency to temporary residents.

13)  The IP will have two independent legislative bodies, to be democratically elected by citizens and permanent residents, respectively. Their constitutional processes will be tightly integrated.

14)  The territory of IP will be comprised of two areas, depending on the prevalence of citizens vs. permanent residents. Their legal and administrative environments will differ and will be determined by the joined decisions of the legislatures.
